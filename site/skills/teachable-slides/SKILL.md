# Teachable Branded Google Slides — How to Use

This skill helps you create on-brand presentations using Teachable's Google Slides template. When helping someone build or review slides, use all of the guidance below to ensure the output looks correct and stays consistent with the Teachable brand.

## The Template

The master template lives here:
**https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1DYaMclnhCkcu9n_PcG8Kdj14SSpo56VVw2tLUTg4kGM/edit**

- **Slides 1 is a warning slide.** It says "Stop! Please make a copy of this deck before anything else." — do not use or alter it.
- **Slides 2–10 are instruction/reference slides.** Do not use or alter these.
- **Slides 11 onward are template slides** that can be freely copied and repurposed for your presentation.

## Basic Usage

- **Duplicate a slide:** Command + D (Mac) to copy any template slide
- **Add slides from the Theme:** Click the **+** button at the top left of Google Slides
- Fill in content, then delete any slides you don't need once the presentation is complete
- Add photos from the stock image library found at the very last pages of the deck
- **Page numbers/footer:** Copy the footer from slides 9 or 10 (Cmd+C) and paste it onto any slide that needs a footer or page number

### Critical: Lemon contrast rule

**Lemon (#E6FF32) fails WCAG accessibility contrast when used as text or icons on a white or light background.** It only has sufficient contrast on dark backgrounds.

| Use Lemon on... | ✅ / ❌ |
|---|---|
| Black background | ✅ Great — high contrast |
| Navy (#1C4774) background | ✅ Great — high contrast |
| Bordeaux (#711014) background | ✅ Great — high contrast |
| White background (as text, arrows, numbers, icons) | ❌ Fails — too low contrast |
| Light Lemon, Cool Beige, Warm Gray backgrounds | ❌ Fails — too low contrast |

**On light/white slides, use Black, Navy, or Charcoal for text, numbers, and icons.** Reserve Lemon for decorative shapes, full-color backgrounds, pills/badges, and accent bars where it appears as a filled block of color (not as the text color itself).

## Typography

Teachable uses two typefaces. Both are available as Google Fonts.

| Brand name | Google Slides / Fonts name | Use for |
|---|---|---|
| Ivy Headline | **Merriweather** | Headlines, titles, impactful statements |
| Peridot PE | **DM Sans** | Body text, labels, captions, UI-style content |

Think about readability at a distance — presentations are often viewed on screens far away. Prioritize larger text and high contrast.

## Logo Usage

Two logo assets are available:
- **Teachable wordmark** — the full "teachable" text logo
- **Teachable icon** — the stylized bracket "t" mark

Both come in dark (black) and light (white/lemon) versions.

**Rules:**
- Light (white or lemon) logo versions should only be used on **dark backgrounds** for readability
- Dark (black) logo version should only be used on **light backgrounds**
- Always maintain clear space around the wordmark
- Never crowd the wordmark with images, text, or other graphics

**Brand tagline:** "You've lived it. Now teach it." — appears on closing/thank you slides.

## Slide Types & When to Use Them

### Cover / Title slides
Eight gradient and solid background options are available — use one to open any presentation:

| Background | Best for |
|---|---|
| Lemon (bright yellow) | High-energy, bold openers |
| Navy (dark blue) | Professional, technical talks |
| White/cream | Clean, minimal tone |
| Cool Beige gradient | Warm, approachable feel |
| Warm Gray gradient | Neutral, versatile |
| Black | Premium, high-impact |
| Bordeaux (dark red) | Rich, editorial feel |
| Teal/blue gradient | Dynamic, colorful |

Each cover slide has a large title placeholder, date, and presenter name field. The Teachable wordmark appears at the bottom.

### Thank You slides
Four "Thank you" variants match the cover slide styles. Use one to close your presentation. All include the tagline "You've lived it. Now teach it." and the website URL.

### Contents / Table of Contents
Dark navy background with numbered section items and slide numbers on the right. Use for longer decks to orient your audience.

### Hosted by / Agenda slide
Split layout: presenter photo and name on the left (name in a Lemon label), agenda items on the right with descriptions. Use near the start of webinars and presentations.

### Meet the Team
Grid of headshot photos with name and title below each. Use to introduce multiple team members.

### Statement slides (black background)
White body text on a black background, with Lemon used to highlight a single key word or phrase. Use for high-impact single statements or quotes. Multiple layout variants available.

### Section intro slides with decorative shape
A large abstract letterform (lemon-colored) paired with a headline. Use **sparingly** — only to introduce a new major section. Do not add images or too much text alongside the shape. Too many shape slides in one deck feels overwhelming.

### Standard content slides
White or light background with black text. Use these for the bulk of your content.

Variants include:
- **Headline + body text** (text only, single column)
- **Headline + body + bullet points with icons**
- **Two-column layouts** (text left, image right, or vice versa)
- **Product update** (screenshot + bullets)
- **The Platform / feature grid** (numbered items with icons)
- **Key features** (text left, feature list right)

### Data / stats slides
- **Single large stat** (e.g., "53%") on a Lemon background — use when one number is the whole story
- **Multi-stat / "Proof and momentum"** (multiple large numbers in a grid on light background)
- **Stats + subheadlines** (colored accent boxes with numbers)

### Quote slides
Three styles available:
- Lemon background, large quote, dark text
- Dark background, large quote, light text
- Photo background with quote overlay and attribution

### Agenda / Project slides
- **Project objectives** (numbered goals in circles)
- **RACI matrix** (responsibility assignment table)
- **Project roadmap** (horizontal timeline with phase descriptions)
- **Important lessons** (numbered list with headers)
- **Key learnings & opportunities** (bold statement on navy)

### Q&A slide
Navy background with "Q&A" heading and supporting text. Use to open discussion at the end of a presentation.

### Case study slide
Light background with photo, title, and long-form text. Use for in-depth customer or project stories.

### Founder's story
Navy background with photo, name, and bio. Use for personal narrative slides.

### Call to action / Join
Dark slide with headline, supporting copy, QR code placeholder, and Teachable logo. Use to close with a conversion goal.

## Accessibility Checklist

Before finalizing any presentation, verify the following:

- **Keep slides simple.** Avoid too many graphics, text blocks, or animations on one slide. Never use flashing animations.
- **Maximize contrast and text size.** High contrast and large text makes slides easier to read. In particular: never use Lemon (#E6FF32) as a text or icon color on white or light backgrounds — it fails contrast checks. On light slides, use Black, Navy, or Charcoal instead. Lemon is safe as text only on dark backgrounds (black, navy, bordeaux).
- **Explain data visually.** If a slide has a chart or graph, state the key takeaway on the slide or in speaker notes. Don't rely on color alone to convey meaning.
- **Add alt text to images.** Select the image → right-click → "Alt text."
- **Caption videos.** Enable closed captions in Zoom, Google Slides, or Google Meet.
- **Screen reader support.** Press Ctrl + Alt + Z in Google Slides to activate screen reader compatibility.
- **Share in advance.** Circulate the deck before the meeting so attendees can review it. If sharing isn't possible, send a text outline instead.
- **Define jargon.** If you use acronyms or technical terms, include a glossary slide.

For more detail, consider using an [accessibility checker extension for Google Slides](https://workspace.google.com/marketplace/search/accessibility).
